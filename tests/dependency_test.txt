1. Generate the "Memory Trigger" Dataset
We want a sequence like: X-------------------X.
The middle characters should be random noise so the model can't "cheat" by learning a pattern. It has to physically store the 'X' in its hidden state/cell state.

2. Modify the Training Loop for "Many-to-One"
In your previous experiments, you did "Many-to-Many" (predicting every character). For this research test, we only care about the final prediction.

3. The Research Protocol (The Loop)
To get a proper research graph, you need to run this for different lengths. This is where you will see the "Memory Wall."

4. What you will see (The Vanishing Gradient Visualization)
When you plot lengths (x-axis) vs accuracy (y-axis):

Standard RNN: Accuracy will be 100% at length 10, then drop to random chance (50% for two triggers) very quickly. This is your mathematical proof that gradients have vanished.

GRU: Will stay at 100% much longer than the RNN but will eventually "forget" around length 80-100.

LSTM: Should hold 100% accuracy the longest. If it fails, it’s usually because the sequence is so long that even the cell state is getting diluted by noise.
